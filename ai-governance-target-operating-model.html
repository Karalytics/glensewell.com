Building an AI Governance Target Operating Model

By Glen Sewell | Published June 2026

Policies, standards, and governance frameworks establish direction. A Target Operating Model translates those requirements into day-to-day execution by defining accountability, processes, technology enablement, reporting, and oversight across the AI lifecycle.

Many organizations begin their AI governance journey by creating policies, principles, and control requirements. While these components are essential, they are rarely sufficient on their own.

Governance only creates value when it becomes operational. Without a clear operating model, accountability becomes fragmented, oversight becomes inconsistent, and governance activities struggle to scale across the enterprise.

Why Operating Models Matter

A Target Operating Model defines how governance functions in practice. It establishes how decisions are made, who is accountable, how risks are managed, how performance is measured, and how governance activities are embedded into business operations.

Effective AI governance requires more than policy. It requires a structure that enables consistent execution.
